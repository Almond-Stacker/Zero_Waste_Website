Polystyrene Foam Ordinance

Expanded polystyrene foam (EPS) — commonly known as Styrofoam — is banned countywide for food service use. The lightweight material breaks into microplastics that persist for centuries, polluting our beaches and reefs.

The law

Ordinance Overview

Bill 59 was passed by the Hawai'i County Council in September 2017 and took effect July 1, 2019. Amendments took effect July 19, 2019. The ordinance prohibits food vendors from using expanded polystyrene foam containers.

1
What's Banned: Expanded polystyrene foam (EPS) food service containers — including plates, cups, bowls, clamshells, and takeout containers — used by food vendors operating in Hawai'i County.
2
Who It Applies To: Restaurants, food trucks, farmers market vendors, caterers, cafeterias, and any entity that provides food for consumption in Hawai'i County. All County permit holders using County facilities must comply.
3
Required Alternatives: Food vendors must use recyclable or compostable food service ware. Preferred options include paper, cardboard, bamboo, sugarcane bagasse, PLA, and other certified compostable materials.
4
County Facilities: All users of County facilities that require a County permit must use suitable recyclable or compostable disposable food service ware at their events and operations.
5
Fines: Violations may result in fines of $500 for the first offense, $1,000 for the second offense, and $2,000 for each subsequent violation.

Quick reference

Banned vs. Acceptable Containers

When in doubt, choose materials that can be composted or recycled. When sourcing new containers, look for certified compostable (BPI or TÜV certified) or easily recyclable options.

❌ Banned — EPS Foam Items

  • 🥡Foam clamshell takeout containers
  • 🍽️Foam plates & trays
  • ☕Foam cups (coffee, soup, cold drinks)
  • 🥣Foam bowls
  • 🍱Foam hinged-lid containers
  • 🧊Foam coolers used to serve food

✅ Acceptable Alternatives

  • 🌿Sugarcane / bagasse containers
  • 🎋Bamboo plates & bowls
  • 📄Paper / cardboard containers
  • 🌱PLA (corn starch) bioplastics
  • 🔄Recyclable plastic (PP, HDPE)
  • 🫙Glass or ceramic (dine-in)

The impact

Why EPS Foam Is Harmful

EPS foam is lightweight and fragile — it breaks apart easily into small beads that travel great distances on wind and water. It never fully biodegrades, only breaking into smaller and smaller pieces.

💨
Easily Blown as Litter

EPS foam breaks apart and travels on the wind, ending up in streams, storm drains, and ultimately the ocean — even when disposed of properly.

🔬
Becomes Microplastics

Polystyrene degrades into microplastics that enter the food chain. These particles have been detected in Hawai'i fish, sea salt, and drinking water.

🐟
Harms Marine Life

Fish and seabirds mistake foam beads for food. The material accumulates in their digestive systems and can cause starvation and internal injury.

🚯
Cannot Be Recycled

EPS foam is not accepted in any recycling program in Hawai'i County. Every foam container becomes permanent landfill waste — or worse, ends up in the environment.
