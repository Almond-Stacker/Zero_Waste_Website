HI-5 Redemption

The Hawai‘i Deposit Beverage Container Program (HI-5) is a State of Hawai‘i funded program that places a 5¢ redeemable deposit on each qualified beverage container. Instead of just tossing your recyclables, why not save them and bring them to one of the 15 convenient redemption centers located island wide? Just look for the HI 5¢ label on your glass, plastic and metal containers, and take them to any redempion center to get cash back and help protect the environment at the same time.

How to Recycle Your HI-5 Containers

Follow these simple steps to prepare your beverage containers for redemption and get your 5¢ deposit back.

1

Check the Label

Look for the HI-5 mark on beverage containers: soft drinks, soda, juice, tea, coffee drinks, water, beer, malt beverages, mixed spirits and wine coolers in metal, glass or plastic containers up to 68 fluid ounces.

2

Rinse & Empty

Empty all remaining liquid and give each container a quick rinse. Get rid of any debris or residue. Clean containers are easier to process and help keep redemption centers sanitary.

3

Sort by Material

Separate your containers into three groups: aluminum, glass, plastic, and bi-metal. This helps redemption centers process your items more efficiently.

4

Check Labels & Remove Caps

Ensure all labels are intact and remove any caps or lids. Complying with Hawai’i Administrative Rules Section 11-282-61, caps should not be included when redeeming by weight. Also caps may be made of different materials and currently not collected for recycling by Hawai’i County.

5

Bag & Transport

Place sorted containers into sturdy bags or bins. Keep material types separate so they can be quickly counted at the redemption center.

6

Visit a Redemption Center

Bring your containers to any of the 15 island-wide redemption centers. Staff will count your items and pay you 5¢ per eligible container on the spot.

Tips for Success

  • Crushed cans are accepted, but labels must still be readable.
  • Containers purchased out of state are not eligible for HI-5 redemption.
  • Most centers are open from 8 AM to 3 PM. Check hours before visiting.
