Matson Foundation program

Ka Ipu 'Āina — Container for the Land

Ka Ipu 'Āina is a Hawaiian expression meaning "container for the land." The program, established in 2001 and funded by the Matson Foundation, provides grants and equipment to nonprofit groups organizing community cleanups across Hawai'i.

🗑️
Container Equipment

Matson donates large container equipment for up to 3 days to support cleanup efforts — perfect for beach cleanups, roadside events, and community park restoration.

💰
$1,000 Charity Donation

Upon successful completion of a cleanup event, a $1,000 check is sent to the organizing nonprofit organization as a thank-you for their community service.

🌺
Hawai'i Island Focus

Active on Oahu, Maui, Kauai, and Hawai'i Island. Community groups across the Big Island are eligible to apply for program support.

🤝
Priority for First-Timers

First-time applicants and groups that have not participated in the program for 3 or more years receive priority consideration for grants.

Who can apply

Eligibility Requirements

Ka Ipu 'Āina is designed for community nonprofit organizations committed to keeping Hawai'i clean. Here's who qualifies.

1
IRS 501(c)(3) Nonprofit Status — Your organization must be a registered nonprofit with federal 501(c)(3) status. Schools, civic clubs, and faith organizations with nonprofit status are eligible.
2
One Project Per Year — Each nonprofit organization may participate in one Ka Ipu 'Āina project per calendar year.
3
Community Cleanup Scope — Projects must involve community volunteers cleaning public or community land — beaches, parks, roadsides, waterways, or trails.
4
Priority for New Applicants — Organizations applying for the first time or that haven't participated in 3 or more years receive priority consideration.

Take action

Other Ways to Get Involved

You don't need to organize a formal event to make a difference. Here are ways individuals and groups can help keep Hawai'i Island clean every day.

🏖️
Beach Cleanups

Join a regular scheduled beach cleanup in your area. Bring gloves, bags, and sunscreen. Check the Events Calendar for upcoming dates.

🛣️
Adopt a Highway

Groups can adopt a stretch of highway through the Hawai'i DOT program, committing to regular cleanups and receiving recognition signs along the route.

📸
Report Illegal Dumping

See something? Report it. Illegal dumping reports help DEM dispatch cleanup crews quickly. Use our online report form.

🌱
Volunteer with Local Groups

Organizations like the Surfrider Foundation, Sierra Club Hawai'i Chapter, and neighborhood associations run regular cleanups open to all volunteers.

📞
Organize a Cleanup — We Can Help

Contact the County DEM at (808) 961-8270 or email swd@hawaiicounty.gov for assistance coordinating cleanup logistics, waste disposal, and community outreach. We're proud to support community action across Hawai'i Island.
