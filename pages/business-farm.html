Hazardous Waste

Business & Farm Hazardous Waste

By law, the County of Hawai'i cannot accept hazardous waste from businesses, agencies, farms, or nonprofits. Commercial generators must use licensed private haulers and follow State regulations.

County cannot accept commercial waste State DOH resources available Pesticide disposal program
⚠️
Commercial Waste Is Not Accepted at County Facilities

Under Hawai'i State law, the County's Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) program and Drop-Off Recycling system are for residential households only. Businesses, nonprofits, farms, and agencies must arrange private disposal through licensed commercial hazardous waste haulers.

Who this applies to

Commercial Hazardous Waste Generators

If you generate hazardous waste through business, farming, or agency operations — even in small quantities — you are a regulated commercial generator and must comply with State requirements.

🏢
Businesses

Restaurants, auto shops, print shops, cleaning services, medical offices, salons, and any business generating hazardous waste as part of operations.

🌾
Farms & Agriculture

Pesticides, herbicides, fertilizers, and petroleum products from agricultural operations require licensed commercial disposal — not the County HHW program.

🏛️
Government Agencies & Nonprofits

Even tax-exempt organizations and government entities must use commercial disposal channels for hazardous waste generated through operations.

🏫
Schools & Institutions

Laboratory chemicals, cleaning supplies used in bulk, and other institutional hazardous materials fall under commercial generator rules.

Where to go

State of Hawai'i Resources

The Hawai'i Department of Health Hazardous Waste Section maintains listings of licensed service providers for commercial hazardous waste management.

1
Hazardous Materials Management Companies — Licensed firms that collect, transport, treat, and dispose of commercial hazardous waste. Contact the Hawai'i DOH Solid & Hazardous Waste Branch at (808) 974-4000 ext. 64226 for an up-to-date list.
2
Hazardous Waste Transporters — State-licensed companies authorized to transport commercial hazardous waste from your facility. A current transporters list is available from the Hawai'i DOH.
3
Used Oil Transporters — Businesses generating used motor oil (auto shops, equipment operators) must use a licensed used-oil transporter. List available from Hawai'i DOH.
4
Universal Waste Collectors — Fluorescent lamps, batteries, pesticides, and certain electronics may qualify as universal waste with simplified collection procedures. See Hawai'i DOH Universal Waste rules.

For farms & agricultural operations

Pesticide Disposal Program

The State of Hawai'i Department of Agriculture operates a Pesticide Disposal Program that helps farmers and agricultural operations properly dispose of unwanted or outdated pesticides.

🌿

What's Covered

Program Pesticide Disposal — no charge up to 250 lbs
Eligibility Farms, ranches, agricultural operations
Items Outdated, unwanted, or unusable pesticides and herbicides

Know the consequences

Illegal Dumping of Commercial Waste

Illegally dumping commercial hazardous waste is a serious violation of both County and State law. The penalties are severe and cleanup costs can be assessed back to the generator.

!
Civil fines of up to $25,000 per day per violation under the Hawai'i Hazardous Waste Law (HRS Chapter 342J).
!
Criminal penalties including fines and imprisonment for knowing violations, especially for large-quantity generators.
!
Cleanup liability — generators can be held responsible for all remediation costs if hazardous waste contaminates soil, groundwater, or the ocean.
