Hazardous Waste

Household Hazardous Waste

Paints, pesticides, batteries, and cleaning chemicals should never go in the trash or down the drain. Bring them to a free County HHW collection event — protecting our 'āina starts at home.

Free drop-off events No appointment needed Residents only Stay in your vehicle
☣️
For Residents Only

County HHW events are available to Hawai'i County residents at no charge. Businesses and farms with hazardous waste must use a licensed commercial hazardous waste hauler. Do not mix household and commercial waste.

What's accepted

Accepted & Not Accepted Materials

Items must be in their original, labeled containers when possible. Unlabeled or unknown materials cannot be accepted for safety reasons.

✅ Accepted (Free)

  • 🎨Paint & Stain (latex & oil-based)
  • 🌿Pesticides & Herbicides
  • 🔋Batteries (all types)
  • 🧴Household Cleaners & Degreasers
  • 🪣Pool Chemicals
  • 🧪Solvents & Thinners
  • ⛽Gasoline & Fuel Additives
  • 🔦Fluorescent & CFL Bulbs
  • 🧯Fire Extinguishers
  • 💊Pharmaceuticals & Medications
  • 🖨️Printer & Toner Cartridges
  • 🪫Propane Tanks (small, ≤20 lb)

❌ Not Accepted

  • 💥Explosives & Fireworks
  • ☢️Radioactive Materials
  • 🔫Ammunition & Weapons
  • 🚗Business / Commercial Waste
  • 🛢️Large Propane Tanks (>20 lb)
  • 📺Electronics (use E-Waste program)
  • 🛞Tires (use Transfer Station)
  • ⚗️Unknown or Unlabeled Chemicals

Not sure if your item is accepted? Call the DEM office at (808) 961-4354 before your visit. Staff are happy to help identify your material and advise on disposal options.

At the event

Safety Rules & Drop-Off Instructions

These rules protect you, staff, and other residents. Please follow all instructions from site attendants.

🚗
Stay in Your Vehicle

Remain inside your car at all times. Trained staff will safely unload your items — do not exit the vehicle.

🏷️
Original Containers Only

Bring materials in their original, labeled containers whenever possible. Materials in food containers or unmarked bottles cannot be accepted.

📦
Pack Upright & Sealed

Place containers upright in a box or tray to prevent spills during transport. Secure lids tightly before loading your vehicle.

🚫
No Mixing

Never mix different chemicals together. Keep each container separate. Mixed or unknown chemicals pose serious safety hazards.

🧤
Ventilate Your Vehicle

Open windows when transporting chemicals. If you notice strong fumes, pull over safely in a ventilated area before continuing.

⏰
Arrive Before Close

The line closes at the listed end time. Arriving early reduces wait time. Events run until materials are filled to capacity.

Everyday choices

Reduce Hazardous Waste at Home

The best hazardous waste is the kind you never generate. Small changes in purchasing habits make a big difference.

1
Buy only what you need. Purchase the smallest quantity of a product that will do the job. Leftover chemicals are the biggest source of household hazardous waste.
2
Choose non-toxic alternatives. Many cleaning, pest control, and garden tasks can be accomplished with vinegar, baking soda, or plant-based products that are safer for your family and our ocean.
3
Share or donate leftover product. Usable paint, pool chemicals, and garden supplies can often be donated to neighbors, community groups, or Habitat for Humanity's ReStore.
4
Use it up before buying more. Check your storage area before purchasing. Many households already have what they need — buried under newer purchases.
5
Read product labels. Labels include proper use, storage, and disposal instructions. Following them protects you and ensures safe disposal at end-of-life.
