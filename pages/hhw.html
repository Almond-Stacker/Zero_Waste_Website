Household Hazardous Waste Collection

Paints, pesticides, cleaners, batteries, and other hazardous household products cannot go in the regular trash. The County of Hawaiʻi holds free drive-thru HHW collection events in Hilo and Kona throughout the year — no appointment needed.

Safety Rules — Read Before You Arrive

These rules protect you, your family, and the event workers. Non-compliance may result in being turned away.

🚗
Stay In Your Vehicle

You must remain inside your vehicle at all times during the event. Trained staff will approach your car to unload materials. Do not exit the vehicle until directed by staff.

📦
Items in Trunk or Truck Bed

All HHW materials must be placed in your trunk, hatchback, or truck bed before arriving — not on the back seat, passenger footwell, or front seat. Keep them upright and separated.

🐾
No Pets Outside the Vehicle

Pets must remain inside the vehicle at all times during the event. Animals may be startled by fumes, equipment, or staff movement in the collection area.

👶
Children Stay Inside

Children must remain seated inside the vehicle throughout the collection process. The event site is an active work area with potentially hazardous materials and moving vehicles.

🏷️
Keep Original Labels When Possible

Do not transfer products into unmarked containers. Bring materials in their original containers. If labels are missing, write a description on the container so staff can identify it safely.

🚫
Do Not Mix Products

Never mix different chemical products together, even in the same bag. Certain combinations can cause violent reactions, fires, or toxic fumes. Each product must be in its own closed container.

Overview

What Is Household Hazardous Waste?

Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) refers to leftover household products that contain corrosive, toxic, ignitable, or reactive ingredients. Common examples include paints, cleaners, oils, batteries, and pesticides. When these products are improperly disposed of — poured down drains, dumped in storm drains, or placed in regular trash — they can pollute groundwater, harm aquatic ecosystems, and endanger sanitation workers.

The County of Hawaiʻi holds HHW collection events regularly in Hilo and Kona. These events are free for residents and operated as a drive-thru service — you never have to leave your vehicle. Materials are then transported to licensed hazardous waste disposal facilities on the mainland.

Before You Go

How to Prepare for the Event

A little preparation at home makes the event run smoothly for everyone. Follow these steps the day before you plan to attend.

1
Inventory Your Materials

Walk through your garage, laundry room, and storage areas. Identify any products that are flammable, corrosive, toxic, or say "Caution," "Warning," or "Danger" on the label.

2
Keep Original Containers

Leave all materials in their original, labeled containers. Do not consolidate products or transfer them to unlabeled jars or bottles. If the original label is missing, write what it is with a marker.

3
Check for Leaks

Inspect each container for leaks or cracks. Place leaking containers inside a sealed plastic bag or a secondary container to prevent spills during transport.

4
Load in Trunk or Truck Bed

Place all HHW materials in your trunk or truck bed before driving to the event. Items must not be in the passenger compartment. Keep them upright and stable.

5
Arrive Early

Events typically run 8 AM–12 PM but may close early when capacity is reached. Arriving within the first hour is recommended, especially for larger loads.

6
Follow Staff Instructions

Event workers will direct you through the line. Stay in your vehicle, pop your trunk when asked, and allow staff to handle all unloading. Do not exit your vehicle unless specifically directed.

  • Wood stains, varnish & lacquer
  • Shellac & primer
  • Paint thinner & turpentine
  • Stripper & remover
🧹 Household Cleaners & Solvents
  • Oven cleaner & drain opener
  • Toilet bowl cleaner (acid-based)
  • Rust remover & metal polish
  • Acetone & mineral spirits
  • Adhesives & contact cement
🌿 Pesticides & Herbicides
  • Insecticides & bug sprays
  • Weed killers & herbicides
  • Rodenticides & rat poison
  • Fungicides & algaecides
  • Flea & tick products
🚗 Automotive Products
  • Motor oil & used oil filters
  • Antifreeze & coolant
  • Brake fluid & transmission fluid
  • Carburetor cleaner & degreasers
  • Auto wax & rust inhibitors
💡 Batteries & Bulbs
  • Household alkaline batteries (AA, AAA, C, D, 9V)
  • Rechargeable batteries (NiMH, NiCd, Li-ion)
  • Button & coin cell batteries
  • Compact fluorescent bulbs (CFLs)
  • Fluorescent tubes (all lengths)
  • Mercury thermometers & thermostats
🔥 Pool Chemicals & Propane
  • Pool chlorine tablets & shock
  • Muriatic acid (pool acid)
  • Small propane cylinders (1 lb camping size)
  • Fire extinguishers (spent or expired)
🚫

NOT Accepted at HHW Events

The following items cannot be accepted at any HHW collection event for safety, regulatory, or logistical reasons.

💣 Explosives & Ammunition
  • Ammunition & bullets of any caliber
  • Flares & fireworks (contact Police Dept.)
  • Blasting caps & explosive compounds
  • Gunpowder (contact Police Dept.)
☢️ Radioactive & Medical Waste
  • Radioactive materials of any kind
  • Syringes & sharps (use Sharps disposal program)
  • Infectious or biohazardous waste
  • Prescription medications (use Drug Take-Back)
  • Chemotherapy drugs
🏭 Commercial & Industrial Waste
  • Commercial business quantities
  • Farm or agricultural chemicals in bulk
  • PCBs (polychlorinated biphenyls)
  • Asbestos-containing materials
  • Large propane tanks (over 1 lb)
♻️ Items With Other Programs
  • Lead-acid car batteries (free at transfer stations)
  • Electronic waste — TVs, computers (see Electronics page)
  • Tires (tire recycling program, see Specialty page)
  • Scrap metal & appliances (see Metals & Appliances page)
⚠️ Special Cases
  • Unknown or unidentified substances without any label
  • Materials in leaking containers with no secondary containment
  • Mixed or combined chemicals in a single container

Convenient Option

Used Motor Oil — Year-Round Drop-Off

You do not need to wait for an HHW event to dispose of used motor oil. The County of Hawaiʻi accepts used motor oil and oil filters at all transfer stations year-round during normal operating hours at no charge. Many auto parts retailers — AutoZone, O'Reilly, and NAPA — also accept used oil for free.

Oil must be free of gasoline, solvents, antifreeze, or other contaminants. Mixed or contaminated oil significantly increases disposal costs and may be rejected. Bring it in a sealed, clearly labeled container. Do not mix oil with antifreeze — each must be disposed of separately.

Businesses — including small businesses, farms, and non-profits — are considered "Conditionally Exempt Small Quantity Generators" under federal and state hazardous waste law and must arrange disposal through licensed commercial hazardous waste haulers. Contact DEM at (808) 961-8270 for referrals to licensed commercial waste disposal companies.
There is no strict quantity limit for most household materials, but the program is designed for household accumulations — not commercial stockpiles. If you have an unusually large quantity (e.g., dozens of gallons of paint or a large collection of chemicals), contact DEM in advance at (808) 961-8270 to discuss your situation before the event.
Collected materials are consolidated, manifested, and shipped to licensed hazardous waste treatment, storage, and disposal facilities (TSDFs) on the mainland under strict federal and state regulations. Reusable materials like latex paint are often blended into usable "recycled paint." Used oil is re-refined or burned as fuel in industrial boilers. Materials that cannot be recycled are incinerated at high-temperature facilities designed to safely destroy hazardous compounds.
Medications and sharps (needles, syringes) are not accepted at HHW events. For prescription medications, use the DEA National Drug Take-Back program — collection boxes are available at most local pharmacies and police stations. For sharps, the County operates a Sharps Collection Program at designated transfer stations. Contact DEM or the Health Department at (808) 974-4000 for current sharps drop-off locations.
