What counts as illegal dumping

Common Dump Site Materials

Illegal dumps are found along roadsides, in forests, on beaches, and on private land. Common materials include:

🛒
Furniture & Appliances Mattresses, sofas, refrigerators, washers
🚗
Vehicles, Tires & Auto Parts Abandoned cars, tire piles, fluid containers
🏗
Construction Debris Concrete, drywall, lumber, roofing, asphalt
⚠
Hazardous Materials Paint, solvents, batteries, oil, chemicals
💻
Electronics TVs, monitors, computers, printers
🚢
Bagged Household Trash General rubbish bags left on roadsides or trails
What to document before you report

Helpful Information

The more detail you provide, the faster DEM can respond. Try to note:

  • ✓ Exact location — address, intersection, or GPS coordinates
  • ✓ Date(s) and time(s) the dumping occurred or was discovered
  • ✓ Type of material(s) and estimated quantity
  • ✓ Vehicle descriptions and license plate numbers (if witnessed)
  • ✓ Photos of the dump site and materials (email to cohdem@hawaiicounty.gov)
  • ✓ Any other identifying markings or details
👁

Reports may be submitted anonymously. Your contact info is optional and will only be used to follow up on your complaint.

Hawaiʻi Revised Statutes §342H

Illegal Dumping Is a Serious Offense

Under state law, everyone — including property owners, contractors, and waste haulers — is responsible for proper waste disposal. Fines apply even if you hired someone else who dumped illegally.

$10,000 per day

Administrative & civil penalties for illegal dumping (HRS §342H-9)

$25,000 per day

Criminal penalties for 1–10 cubic yards of solid waste (HRS §342H-39)

Felony Class C

Knowingly dumping 10+ cubic yards without written DOH approval (HRS §342H-37)

Each day of violation is counted as a separate offense.

Get Involved

Organize a Community Clean-Up

A joint effort by government, landowners, contractors, and residents is the most effective way to address illegal dumping. The County can help.

  • The Matson Foundationʼs Ka Ipu ʻĀina Program provides grants to non-profit groups for community clean-ups.
  • Request a Waiver of Tipping Fees for illegal dump remediation by submitting the fillable form to DEM.
  • Property owners can deter dumping with no dumping signs, fencing, lighting, and landscaping in high-incident areas.
