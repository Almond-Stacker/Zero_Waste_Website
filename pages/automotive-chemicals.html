Hazardous Waste

Automotive & Chemical Waste

Used motor oil, antifreeze, gasoline, and other automotive fluids are toxic to Hawai'i's waterways and reef systems. Dispose of them safely — never down the drain or in the trash.

Used motor oil drop-off Up to 4-gallon limit Residents only HHW events accepted
⚠️
Never Dump Automotive Fluids

One gallon of used motor oil can contaminate up to one million gallons of drinking water. Pouring automotive fluids on the ground or into drains is illegal and harms our ocean, reefs, and freshwater sources.

DIY oil change program

Used Motor Oil Drop-Off

The County operates a DIY used motor oil recycling program for non-business vehicle owners. Drop off up to 4 gallons at participating locations — no appointment needed.

🛢️
4-Gallon Limit

The program accepts up to 4 gallons per visit from household DIY generators only. Commercial quantities are not accepted.

🧴
Container Requirements

Oil must be in a container with a lid and pourable spout. Do not use bleach, antifreeze, or pesticide bottles — these contaminate the oil and make it unrecyclable.

🚫
No Mixing

Do not mix oil with other fluids (antifreeze, gasoline, brake fluid). Mixed oil cannot be recycled and must be treated as hazardous waste.

🏠
Residents Only

This program is for household vehicles only. Businesses, farms, and fleet operators must use a licensed used-oil transporter.

HHW collection events

Automotive Chemicals at HHW Events

The following automotive chemicals are accepted at County Household Hazardous Waste collection events. A combined 10-gallon limit applies — no single container may exceed 5 gallons.

✅ Accepted Automotive Fluids

  • ❄️Antifreeze / Engine Coolant
  • 🛢️Used Motor Oil
  • ⛽Gasoline & Diesel
  • 🔧Lubricants & Gear Oil
  • 🛑Brake Fluid
  • ⛽Fuel Additives
  • 🧹Engine Degreasers & Cleaners
  • 🪫Vehicle Batteries (lead-acid)

❌ Not Accepted at Events

  • 🏭Commercial / business quantities
  • 🛢️Containers larger than 5 gallons
  • ❓Unknown or unlabeled fluids
  • 🔩Vehicle parts & scrap metal
  • 🚗Entire vehicles or transmissions

Keeping our island beautiful

Abandoned & Junk Vehicles

Abandoned cars and tires detract from Hawai'i's natural beauty and contain toxic substances — fuels, metals, oils, and coolants — that wash into waterways and the ocean when it rains.

1
Report abandoned vehicles to Police Dispatch at (808) 935-3311. An officer will attach a 24-hour notice; the vehicle may be removed if not moved.
2
Scrap metal and junk vehicles can be dropped off at County Recycling and Transfer Stations. Contact the facility for size and weight limitations.
3
Tires are accepted at County Transfer Stations. Limit of 4 passenger tires per visit for residential self-haulers. Commercial tire disposal requires a licensed hauler.
