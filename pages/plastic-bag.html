Plastic Bag Reduction Ordinance

Hawai'i County was the first county in the nation to ban single-use plastic checkout bags — a landmark step in protecting our ocean, reefs, and land from one of the most persistent plastic pollutants.

The law

Ordinance Overview

Hawai'i County Code Chapter 20, Article 6 prohibits retail businesses from providing single-use plastic checkout bags to customers at the point of sale.

1
Effective Date: January 17, 2013. All County of Hawai'i businesses must comply — groceries, restaurants, farmer's markets, and all other retailers.
2
What's Banned: Single-use plastic carryout bags (the thin bags typically used at checkout). This includes standard grocery bags and retail shopping bags made of plastic film.
3
Permitted Alternatives: Reusable bags (cloth, canvas, heavy-duty plastic), paper bags made from at least 40% recycled content, and compostable bags meeting ASTM D6400 standards.
4
Exemptions: Produce bags, bulk food bags, newspaper bags, dry-cleaning bags, and bags used to contain raw meat or fish. Bags provided by 501(c)(3) nonprofits are also exempt.
5
Fines: Violations may result in fines of $100 to $1,000 per violation. Repeat offenses can result in higher penalties and business license action.

Easy swaps

Alternatives to Plastic Bags

Making the switch is simple — and it saves money over time. Here's what to use instead.

🛍️
Reusable Canvas or Cloth Bags

Keep several in your car, by the door, or in your purse. One canvas bag replaces hundreds of plastic bags over its lifetime.

🧺
Reusable Produce Bags

Lightweight mesh bags work perfectly for fruits, vegetables, and bulk items. Washable and long-lasting.

📦
Paper Bags

Recyclable paper bags made from recycled content are an acceptable alternative at checkout. Many stores provide them for a small fee.

🎒
Backpacks & Tote Bags

Use what you already own. A daypack or tote works just as well for smaller shopping trips.

The impact

Why Plastic Bags Are Harmful

Plastic bags are one of the most common items found in ocean cleanups. Their light weight means they travel far and degrade slowly — posing a persistent threat to marine life and coral reefs.

🐢
Marine Wildlife

Sea turtles, dolphins, and seabirds mistake plastic bags for jellyfish and other food. Ingestion causes internal injuries, starvation, and death.

🪸
Coral Reef Damage

Plastic bags draped over coral reefs block sunlight and introduce harmful bacteria, killing the coral and the ecosystems that depend on it.

🔬
Microplastics

Over decades, plastic bags break into microplastics that enter the food chain — from plankton to fish to the plate. They've been found in Hawai'i seafood, rain, and drinking water.

🏖️
Beach Pollution

Hawai'i's beaches are among the most beautiful in the world — and among the most plastic-polluted in the Pacific. Every bag kept out of the waste stream helps protect them.

📧
Report a Violation

If a business is still providing single-use plastic checkout bags, report it to the County at bring-ur-bag@hawaiicounty.gov. Include the business name, address, and date of the incident. For questions, call DEM at (808) 961-8270.
