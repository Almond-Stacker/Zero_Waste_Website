Recycle & Dispose

Drop-Off Recycling

Hawaiʻi County's free drop-off recycling program diverts glass, paper, metals, and plastic from the landfill. Use the two-bin system at any County transfer station — it's fast, easy, and free.

Free at all transfer stations 2-bin system: Fiber & Commingled No appointment needed
ℹ️
Clean, Dry & Loose — 3 Rules to Remember

All recyclables must be clean (rinsed free of food residue), dry (no standing liquid inside), and loose — not bagged in plastic. Plastic bags jam sorting equipment and contaminate entire loads. If in doubt, leave it out.

How It Works

Three Simple Steps to Recycle

Follow these three steps every time and you will keep the program running cleanly and efficiently for everyone on the island.

1
🚿

Rinse

Give containers a quick rinse with water. Remove food scraps, oils, and liquids. Labels can stay on — they are removed during processing. Lids can remain on plastic bottles.

2
🗂️

Sort

Separate into two streams: Fiber (paper and cardboard) and Commingled (glass, cans, and plastic). Flatten cardboard boxes. Keep everything loose — no plastic bags.

3
♻️

Drop

Bring sorted materials to any County transfer station. Place fiber items in the blue/paper bin and commingled items in the commingled bin. Transfer stations are open six to seven days a week.

What Goes Where

The 2-Bin System

Hawaiʻi County uses a source-separated system with two distinct streams. Mixing them together causes contamination and sends recyclables to the landfill.

📄

Bin 1 — Fiber

Paper & Cardboard stream

  • ✓Newspaper & inserts
  • ✓Magazines & catalogs
  • ✓Office paper & envelopes (including windows)
  • ✓Junk mail & flyers
  • ✓Cardboard boxes (flattened)
  • ✓Paper bags & paper grocery bags
  • ✓Paperboard / chipboard (cereal, shoe, cracker boxes)
  • ✓Phone books & directories
  • ✓Paper tubes (paper towel, toilet paper cores)
  • ✓Wrapping paper (non-foil, non-glitter)
Tip: Flatten all cardboard to save space in the bin and make transport easier. Remove excessive tape or foam packing materials from boxes.
🫙

Bin 2 — Commingled

Glass, cans & plastic stream

  • ✓Glass bottles & jars (all colors, all sizes)
  • ✓Aluminum beverage cans
  • ✓Steel & tin cans (food cans, pet food cans)
  • ✓Plastic bottles & jugs (#1–#7, all types)
  • ✓Plastic tubs, cups & containers (yogurt, deli containers)
  • ✓Aluminum foil & foil trays (clean)
  • ✓Empty aerosol cans (completely empty, no liquid)
  • ✓Cardboard juice & milk cartons (aseptic & gable-top)
  • ✓Tetra Pak / drink boxes (rinsed)
  • ✓Metal lids & bottle caps
Tip: Rinse all containers so they are free of food residue. Lids can stay on plastic bottles. Crush aluminum cans if desired — do not crush glass.
Keep These Out

What Is NOT Accepted

The following items contaminate recyclable loads and cause entire batches to be sent to the landfill. When in doubt, throw it out.

✗Plastic bags & film
✗Styrofoam / EPS foam
✗Food waste & soiled containers
✗Liquids (drain before recycling)
✗Window glass & mirrors
✗Ceramics, Pyrex, drinking glasses
✗Light bulbs (use HHW program)
✗Batteries (use HHW program)
✗Wax-coated cardboard
✗Greasy pizza boxes
✗Shredded paper (in bags)
✗Medical syringes / sharps
✗Electronics (see electronics page)
✗Yard waste & food scraps
✗Clothing & textiles
✗Construction materials / debris
♻️
Plastic bags are the #1 contaminant

Never put recyclables inside a plastic bag — even a bag full of recycling. Empty materials loose into the bins. Plastic bags are accepted separately at most supermarkets (Safeway, KTA, Walmart) under the plastic bag ordinance take-back program.

Shredded paper cannot be placed in the fiber recycling bin loose — the pieces are too small and jam sorting equipment. Place shredded paper in a sealed paper envelope or small cardboard box before putting it in the fiber bin. Do not use plastic bags to contain shredded paper.
It depends on how soiled the box is. If the bottom is heavily soaked with grease, it is not recyclable — put it in the trash. If only the top lid has minor grease staining and the bottom is relatively clean, you can tear the lid off and recycle the clean portion in the fiber bin. Materials collected at transfer stations are consolidated and shipped to processors on the mainland. Paper and cardboard are baled and sold to paper mills. Glass is crushed and used for construction aggregate or shipped off-island. Metals are melted and remanufactured. Plastic is sorted by resin type and processed into new products. The overall program diverts thousands of tons of material from the County landfill each year.
