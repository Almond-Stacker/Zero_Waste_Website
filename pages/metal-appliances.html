Freon Appliances Require a Certificate

Refrigerators, freezers, air conditioners, and dehumidifiers containing Freon (CFC/HFC refrigerants) will not be accepted unless you can provide a signed certificate from an EPA Section 608 Certified technician confirming that the refrigerant has been properly recovered. A $20 disposal fee applies. Appliances without Freon are accepted free of charge.

Scrap Metal

Ferrous and non-ferrous metals accepted free of charge at all County transfer stations. Metals are sorted, baled, and sold to processors — keeping valuable resources out of the landfill.

Scrap metal is one of the most valuable streams in the recycling program. Whether it is old rebar, aluminum window frames, copper pipe, or a broken lawnmower, the County accepts a wide variety of metal at the scrap metal area of every transfer station.

Metal items must be free of hazardous materials — gas tanks and propane cylinders must be empty and purged. Drums or barrels that contained hazardous chemicals are not accepted.

Large quantities from contractors or commercial operations may require coordination with the transfer station staff.

⚙️ Know Your Metal Types
Ferrous Metals (iron-based)
  • ✓Structural steel beams & rebar
  • ✓Iron pipes & plumbing fixtures
  • ✓Steel barrels (empty, non-hazardous)
  • ✓Metal roofing & corrugated iron
  • ✓Old farm equipment parts
  • ✓Lawn mowers & garden tools (no fuel)
  • ✓Metal furniture frames

💡 Ferrous metals stick to a magnet. They are heavier and typically less valuable per pound than non-ferrous.

Non-Ferrous Metals (higher value)
  • ✓Aluminum — cans, siding, frames, foil
  • ✓Copper — pipe, wire, tubing
  • ✓Brass — fixtures, valves, decorative items
  • ✓Stainless steel — sinks, pots, exhaust
  • ✓Lead — old pipe (no paint chips)
  • ✓Zinc — galvanized metal, hardware

💡 Non-ferrous metals do not stick to a magnet. They have higher recycling value and may be eligible for HI-5 refund if purchased in Hawaiʻi.

White Goods — Large Appliances

"White Goods" is the industry term for large household appliances such as refrigerators, washers, dryers, and stoves. Freon-containing units require a certified removal certificate.

Large household appliances (often called "White Goods") contain significant amounts of recyclable steel, aluminum, and copper. The County accepts them at all transfer stations, though appliances containing Freon refrigerants require special handling.

Two categories of appliances: those that contain refrigerants (Freon) — which require an EPA Section 608 technician to remove the refrigerant first — and those that do not contain refrigerants, such as washers, dryers, and stoves.

When in doubt about whether your appliance contains Freon, check the back of the unit for a data plate listing the refrigerant type. Common refrigerants include R-22, R-134a, R-410a, and R-32.

⚠️
Freon Removal Certificate Required

Refrigerators, freezers, air conditioners, dehumidifiers, and water coolers that still contain refrigerant cannot be accepted without a written certificate from a technician certified under EPA Section 608. The certificate must identify the appliance, the technician's certification number, and the date refrigerant was recovered. Bring this document with you to the transfer station. A $20 disposal fee applies even with the certificate.

✓

Accepted — White Goods

  • Refrigerators (with Freon cert + $20 fee)
  • Freezers — chest & upright (with Freon cert + $20 fee)
  • Central & window air conditioners (with Freon cert + $20 fee)
  • Dehumidifiers (with Freon cert + $20 fee)
  • Washing machines — free of charge
  • Clothes dryers (gas & electric) — free
  • Dishwashers — free of charge
  • Electric & gas stoves / ranges — free
  • Water heaters (drained) — free
  • Microwave ovens — free
✗

Not Accepted — White Goods

  • Freon appliances without removal certificate
  • Appliances with hazardous liquids still inside
  • Gas cylinders still attached to appliance
  • Appliances with asbestos insulation
  • Commercial/industrial refrigeration units (contact DEM)
  • PCB-containing equipment (pre-1980 capacitors)

📋How to Prepare
  • Drain water heaters completely before transport
  • Remove all food from refrigerators and freezers
  • Detach the unit from gas lines (gas appliances)
  • Have Freon certificate in hand before arriving
  • Remove doors from refrigerators if transporting open-top (child safety)
  • Secure appliances safely in your vehicle
🔍Finding an EPA 608 Technician

Technicians certified under EPA Section 608 can be found through licensed HVAC companies, appliance repair shops, and refrigerant recovery specialists on the Big Island. Ask the technician for a signed certificate on company letterhead listing their EPA certification number and the date refrigerant was recovered.

Contact DEM at (808) 961-8270 for a referral to local certified technicians.

Junk Vehicles & Auto Bodies

Abandoned or inoperable vehicles can be surrendered to the County. All fluids must be drained prior to drop-off. Vehicles with current registration may also qualify for the state junk vehicle program.

The County of Hawaiʻi accepts junk motor vehicle bodies at designated transfer stations. Auto bodies are compacted and shipped off-island for steel recycling. All fluids — oil, coolant, brake fluid, transmission fluid, and gasoline — must be completely drained before the vehicle can be accepted.

You must present the vehicle title or surrender paperwork at the transfer station gate. For abandoned vehicles found on your property, contact the County Abandoned Vehicle Program at (808) 961-8268.

✓

Accepted — Auto Bodies

  • Cars, trucks, and SUVs (all fluids drained)
  • Motorcycles and mopeds
  • ATV and off-road vehicle frames
  • Boat trailers and utility trailers (metal frame only)
  • Vehicle engine blocks (no fluids)
  • Auto body panels and hoods
  • Wheels and rims (without tires)
✗

Not Accepted — Auto Bodies

  • Vehicles with fluids still inside
  • Catalytic converters detached from vehicle body (contact DEM)
  • Tires (separate Tire Recycling Program applies)
  • Airbag modules not deployed (hazardous propellant)
  • Lead-acid batteries (separate free drop-off program)
  • Vehicles with intact airbag modules

How to Drop Off a Junk Vehicle

1
Drain All Fluids Engine oil, coolant, transmission fluid, brake fluid, and gasoline must be removed by a qualified shop or at home.
2
Remove Battery Take out the lead-acid battery and drop it off separately — it is accepted FREE at any transfer station.
3
Bring Title / ID Present the vehicle title or signed surrender paperwork at the transfer station gate when you arrive.
4
Drop It Off Transfer station staff will direct you to the junk vehicle area. No appointment needed at most stations.
Found an abandoned vehicle on your property? Contact the County Abandoned Vehicle Program for free removal assistance.
