The Zero Waste Hierarchy

Not All Rʼs Are Equal

Zero Waste is built on a priority order. Before you recycle, ask yourself: could you reduce or reuse first? The further up the pyramid, the greater the impact.

🚫
Reduce Buy less, buy better. Choose bulk, durable, and repairable goods. The waste you donʼt create is the waste you never have to manage.
Most Impactful
♾
Reuse Give unwanted items a new home. Repair, donate, swap, or repurpose before throwing away.
♻
Recycle Turn discards into new products — but only after reducing and reusing first. Recycling has limits; the first two Rʼs do not.
Last Resort

What Can You Do?

Zero Waste starts at home and at work. Here are concrete steps for everyone on Hawaiʻi Island.

🏠

For Residents

  • Shop with intention Buy in bulk, bring reusable bags, and choose products with minimal packaging.
  • Compost food scraps Join a community composting program or start a backyard bin — food waste is our largest landfill contributor.
  • Repair before replacing Attend a repair café or learn basic fixes for clothes, electronics, and furniture.
  • Use the Mālama Map Find your nearest drop-off for electronics, appliances, HI-5 bottles, and more.
Get Started →
🏭

For Businesses

  • Conduct a waste audit Identify your top waste streams and find diversion pathways for each material type.
  • Join the Reusable Foodware program Hilo food businesses can partner with DEM to replace single-use serviceware at no extra cost.
  • Comply with Bill 40 Review the single-use plastic restrictions, transition timelines, and approved alternatives.
  • Educate your team Request a free Zero Waste workplace training session from the DEM Education team.

Mālama ʻĀina

Care for the Land
That Sustains Us

The Hawaiian concept of mālama ʻāina — caring for the land — has always been a Zero Waste philosophy. When we reduce what we take and return what we can, we honor the ʻāina and leave a better island for our children.

Every small action compounds. Start today.
