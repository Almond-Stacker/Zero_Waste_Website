Recycle & Dispose

Electronics Recycling

Old electronics left in the landfill leak lead, mercury, and cadmium into our groundwater. Up to 80% can be recycled — and on the Big Island, free drop-off is closer than you think.

Free drop-off available Hilo & Kona locations Donate if it still works
⚠️
Not accepted as electronics

Microwave ovens, fans, vacuums, and toys are not considered electronic waste or appliances for recycling purposes in the County of Hawaiʻi. They are regular rubbish. E-Waste is also not accepted at County Household Hazardous Waste Collection Events.

State program

State Law & Program Information

Background on the Hawaiʻi Electronic Device Recycling & Recovery Law.

The Hawaiʻi Electronic Device Recycling and Recovery Law covers: computers, laptops/tablets with screens larger than 4", printers and fax machines, monitors, VCRs, portable digital music players with memory and battery power, DVD/Blu-ray players and recorders, routers and modems for household use, TVs with viewable screens larger than 9", and electronic device peripherals.

Peripherals include keyboards, mice, external input/output devices, cords, power supplies and adapters for electronic devices, speakers used with computers or TVs, and video game consoles.

The State Legislature passed Act 151 in 2022, which requires manufacturers to fully fund their recycling plans including collection, transportation, and recycling of all covered electronic devices in Hawaiʻi. Plans must include manufacturer recycling goals and provide collection services at least once per month at no cost to consumers in certain population centers, starting January 2023.

In 2025, Act 162 expanded the types of devices accepted for free. The full text of Chapter 339D, Hawaiʻi Revised Statutes is available on the State Legislature website. Retailers are prohibited from selling products from non-registered manufacturers and must inform customers where devices can be returned for recycling.
